You sit down at your desk. There are 47 unread emails waiting. Some are important. Most are not.

What if you could get through them in the time it takes to drink your coffee?

Here's the thing: most emails fall into a handful of categories. Meeting requests. Follow-ups. Status updates. Shipping confirmations. Each one follows a pattern. And patterns are exactly what software is good at handling.

## How it works

The idea is simple. Instead of writing each reply from scratch, you let a tool suggest a draft based on the email you received. You read it, tweak it if needed, and hit send.

Most email services now include this feature - or you can add it with a browser extension or app.

## What this looks like in practice

Here's a real example:

1. Someone emails you asking to schedule a meeting next week
2. The tool looks at your calendar, sees when you're free
3. It writes a polite reply suggesting three time slots
4. You glance at it, make one small change, and send

That process used to take three minutes. Now it takes fifteen seconds.

## Getting started

**Step 1:** Open your email settings and look for "Smart Reply" or "Suggestions."

**Step 2:** Turn it on. Most services have it hidden in a menu somewhere.

**Step 3:** Try it on your next five emails. See how close the suggestions are.

**Step 4:** Edit as needed. The goal isn't perfect replies - it's getting from zero to 80% in one click.

## A note on tone

The best tools let you set the tone of your replies. Formal. Casual. Friendly. Professional. Pick the one that sounds like you. Then every suggestion will feel natural, not robotic.

## The bottom line

You reply to hundreds of emails a month. If each one takes three minutes, that's ten hours. If a tool cuts that to one minute, you save nearly seven hours a month.

Seven hours. Imagine what you could do with that time.
