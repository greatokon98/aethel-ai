A few months ago, I was overwhelmed. Working long hours. Always behind. The kind of busy where you never catch up, you just keep the treadmill running.

Then I started asking one question before every task:

**"Could this be done automatically?"**

That simple question changed everything.

## How the question works

Before I start any recurring task, I pause and ask: is this something I'll do more than once? If the answer is yes, I ask: could software do it for me?

Here are the tasks that I automated in my first week:

*Sending follow-up emails after meetings.* I used to do this manually. Now it happens automatically.

*Organizing incoming files.* Anything I download goes to a folder, gets sorted by type, and old files get cleaned up.

*Backing up my work.* This used to be a manual weekly task. Now it's every hour, without me thinking about it.

*Sorting my inbox.* Emails from certain people get flagged. Newsletters get filed. Receipts get saved.

Each one was a small task - five or ten minutes. But together, they were eating hours every week.

## The ripple effect

The first week, I saved about three hours. That was nice.

But here's what I didn't expect: once I started looking for automation opportunities, I found them everywhere.

By week three, I was saving about six hours a week.

By week eight, it was closer to ten.

Not because I was working differently, but because I had stopped doing things that didn't need doing.

## Start today

Here's your challenge: this week, before every recurring task, ask yourself the question.

"Could this be done automatically?"

The answer might surprise you. And you might get your evening back.
