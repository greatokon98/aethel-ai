Every week there is a new AI tool claiming to revolutionize how creators work. Most are forgettable. Some are genuinely useful. Here are the ones that have earned a permanent spot in our workflow.

## For writing and editing

### Lex
A word processor with AI built in. Not a content generator — a writing companion. Lex offers smart autocomplete, tone adjustments, and a clean, distraction-free editor. It feels like what Google Docs should have become.

**Best for:** Long-form writing, newsletters, and first drafts.

**Honest take:** The AI suggestions are subtle enough that they do not feel intrusive. That is the compliment. It helps without taking over.

### Hemingway Editor Plus
The classic readability tool updated with AI suggestions. It flags complex sentences, passive voice, and hard-to-read paragraphs. The AI version goes further, suggesting specific rewrites.

**Best for:** Editing and polishing.

**Honest take:** Hemingway is ruthless. Your writing will be clearer after running it through. Use it before publishing anything.

## For design and visuals

### Canva AI
Canva has integrated AI throughout its platform: text-to-image generation, magic eraser, background remover, and auto-layout. For creators who are not professional designers, it is indispensable.

**Best for:** Social media graphics, presentations, thumbnails.

**Honest take:** Canva AI is not going to win design awards, but it is fast and accessible. For 90% of creator needs, it is enough.

### RunwayML
Video editing with AI. Runway can remove backgrounds from video, generate new footage from text prompts, smooth shaky footage, and much more. Professional editors use it alongside traditional tools.

**Best for:** Video creators and editors.

**Honest take:** Some features feel like magic. Others are still rough. Test before committing to a workflow.

## For audio and podcasting

### Descript
The all-in-one audio/video editor that transcribes everything and lets you edit by editing text. Delete a word from the transcript and it removes the corresponding audio. It also includes AI voice cloning for fixing flubbed lines.

**Best for:** Podcasters and video creators.

**Honest take:** The text-based editing workflow changes how you think about audio. Once you try it, it is hard to go back.

## For marketing and growth

### Copy.ai
Purpose-built for marketing copy: social posts, email campaigns, ad copy, landing pages. It understands different brand voices and maintains consistency.

**Best for:** Social media managers and email marketers.

**Honest take:** Great for generating options. Less great for final copy. Always edit before posting.

### Opus Clip
AI that watches your long-form videos and automatically extracts highlights as short clips optimized for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. It identifies the most engaging moments and captions them.

**Best for:** Repurposing YouTube videos for short-form platforms.

**Honest take:** The clipping is surprisingly good. The captions sometimes need adjustments. Still, it saves hours of manual editing.

## A practical workflow

Here is how these tools fit together for a typical content creator:

1. **Brainstorm** with ChatGPT
2. **Draft** in Lex
3. **Design visuals** in Canva AI
4. **Record audio** and edit in Descript
5. **Repurpose** video with Opus Clip
6. **Write marketing copy** in Copy.ai
7. **Polish** with Hemingway
8. **Publish**

## The golden rule

AI tools are accelerators, not creators. They are most powerful when you bring the strategy, the expertise, and the voice. The tool just makes it faster.

Pick one tool from this list — just one — and master it before trying the next. That is how you build a workflow that actually sticks.
