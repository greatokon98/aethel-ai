You need an image. Maybe for a presentation. A blog post. A social media update.

You could spend an hour searching stock photo sites, fighting with licensing, and settling for something that's "close enough."

Or you could describe what you want and have it created instantly.

## How it works

The idea is beautifully simple: you type what you want to see, and an image appears.

"A watercolor painting of a cozy reading nook with a cat sleeping on a windowsill."

"A minimalist icon of a coffee cup with steam rising."

"A diagram showing how a newsletter travels from writer to reader."

Each description produces a unique image. No searching. No licensing. No settling.

## What it's good for

**Blog post images.** Every article deserves a good header image.

**Social media graphics.** Stand out instead of using the same templates everyone else uses.

**Presentation visuals.** Make your slides memorable with custom illustrations.

**Concept exploration.** Need to visualize an idea before building it? Describe it first.

**Client mockups.** Show what you mean, not what stock photography is available.

## Tips for better results

**Be specific.** "A person working on a laptop" is fine. "A woman in her thirties working on a laptop in a bright coffee shop, natural light, warm colors" is much better.

**Mention a style.** Watercolor. Minimalist. Photorealistic. Cartoon. Line art. The style makes a huge difference.

**Describe the mood.** Peaceful. Energetic. Professional. Playful.

**Mention colors.** "Warm earth tones" or "cool blues and grays" guides the result.

## The best part

You don't need to be a designer. You don't need to know how to use Photoshop. You don't need to struggle with Canva templates.

You just need to know what you want. And you can describe it in words.
